Introduction brief

Cultural diversity & sustainability

The future TSG is designed around two major focuses: cultural diversity and sustainability. It starts from Australia's Aboriginal culture and expands into a participatory method that encourages visitors to exchange stories, materials, and perspectives.

TSG context

TSG is a physical contemporary exhibition space at USYD. Give & Get keeps the gallery, then adds a digital participation layer.

Archive display method

Donations, exhibitions, archives, bubbles

The archive should show four related layers: existing TSG exhibitions, donated Give & Get items, previous TSG archives, and bubble discussions. The browsing experience is visual, so people can move between objects, exhibition images, and comments instead of reading a flat database.

Example from TSG: Sydney Buries its Past brought Tin Sheds Archive material from 1972-1994 into dialogue with contemporary works. In Give & Get, this becomes a model for connecting past exhibition memory with visitor-donated stories.

Future Virtual TSG

Sustainable archive gallery

This is our imagination and concept for Future TSG: a sustainable archive gallery where visitors bring reusable or recyclable materials as entry tickets. They can donate them or use them to create DIY souvenirs. These works are 3D scanned, archived, shown online, and can be considered for voting or future exhibitions.

Bubble discussion

All sounds from past and now

Bubble records comments, reflections, and small emotional responses from visitors. These voices sit beside the formal archive and help future audiences understand how people responded to cultural objects, TSG archives, and Give & Get stories.

Example: after seeing the donated Aboriginal-pattern skirt in the app, one visitor might describe the pattern as warm and familiar; another might ask how cultural symbols should be shared respectfully; another might connect it to a community memory. These short comments become a softer public archive.

Reflection

Innovation with feasibility

The project originally imagined a completely new gallery and holographic screens, but this was too costly for near-future TSG. The final direction retains TSG's internal and external spaces, then uses AR virtual overlays and online archives to make the future service more feasible.
